Cryptocurrencies of the World

BTC Bitcoin

The first decentralized cryptocurrency, launched in 2009. It runs on a peer-to-peer blockchain, has a fixed supply (21 million coins), and is often used as “digital gold”.

ETH Ethereum

A blockchain platform (since 2015) that supports smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps). ETH is the native token used to power transactions and operations on the network.

USDT Tether

A stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the U.S. dollar (or other fiat) designed to reduce volatility. It’s widely used in crypto markets for trading and as a bridge to fiat.

BNB Binance Coin

The native token of the BNB Chain (by the exchange operator) used to pay fees, access token sales, and participate in the ecosystem. Originally launched by the Binance exchange.
